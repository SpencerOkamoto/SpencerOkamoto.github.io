Project Background

This research project began in the Late Summer of 2020. With covid-19 shining light on supply chain issues and pollution, this project is centered around the most important industry of today. As the Green Revolution progresses, the progress relies on rare earth metals for batteries and other components. My project focused on the interaction of geothermal mines on the beginning of the San Andreas Fault under the Salton Sea. A lesser-documented series of earthquakes were reported in the area as geothermal activities came into the area. These sequences are common in areas such as Alaska and Yellowstone National Park where geothermal gysers are naturally formed. The Salton Sea has a similar story; however, the introduction of geothermal mining is thought to have increased these events. A by-product of this is Lithium, which has brought the industry to begin Lithium mining - a more disruptive type of mining. Most companies inject hot water solutions into the ground to force lithium filled solution up. This injection stresses the nearby geology and potentially cause fault movement.

Python Visualizations

In order to test behavior, USGS data was analyzed and compared to normal earthquake trends. A normal Main Event Sequence has a single point of highest magnitude with the number of events decaying exponentially. I observed these behaviors and how they contrast from what I observed in Westmorland. Omori's Law describes how the number of events decay over time as energy migrates away. Gutenberg-Ritcher's Law describes how magnitude decays as energy leaves. Below are two sequences, one is a Main Event and the other is a Swarm.

Results of My Work

Earthquake Swarms do not obey Omori's or Gutenberg-Ritcher's Law. These are rare occurences, mostly in natural hot springs, where energy builds-up in the surrounding faults and causes strange behaviors. These have the potential to be more devasting by increasing ground waves and prolonging shocks. The danger in the Salton Sea is the potential to cause a large earthquake migrating along the San Andreas Fault into Los Angeles. This would have devastating consequences, yet Lithium mines are already setting-up shop. It is essential to continue research on its effects on the neighboring geology.
