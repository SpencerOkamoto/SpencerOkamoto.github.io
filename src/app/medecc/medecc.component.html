MedECC is a telemedicine web application using Angular and .NET to provide a global network of healthcare professionals to support underserved or remote communities. The app began as a patient monitoring device for understaffed hospitals during Covid-19, then evolved into a broader healthcare solution.

Our team consisted of a front-end team (2-3), back-end team (2), design team (1-2) and project leads (2). Our team worked in a fully remote setting.

Background and Task

MedECC started as a response to overcrowding in hospitals during the covid-19 main surges. Our goal was to create a telehealth solution for remote monitoring expanding hospital resources and trained workers. As vaccines rolled out, the overcrowding problem subsided. I was tasked to rescope the current application into a product better representing our client's needs.

Our Clients were globally distributed in India, Mexico, and Zambia. They consisted of hospital administrators, physicians, and nurses who participated in user research, surveys, and state of development presentations.

Problem the initial prototype was only applicable to the current crisis with no future plans for expansion.
